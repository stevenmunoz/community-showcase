TinyMVP

by Aisha Patel

TinyMVP lets anyone create a landing page with a waitlist and Stripe checkout in under 5 minutes ‚Äî no code required. The idea came from watching indie hackers spend weeks building landing pages instead of validating ideas. TinyMVP strips it down to the essentials: a headline, a description, a price, and a payment button. That's it. Still in prototype with 200 signups, but the core thesis is proving out: the faster you can charge $1, the faster you know if your idea has legs.

The Mom Test
Don't ask 'would you use this?' ‚Äî watch what they do

The Mom Test (from Rob Fitzpatrick's book) teaches that people will lie to be polite. 'Would you use this?' always gets a yes. Instead, ask about their current behavior: 'How do you solve this problem today?' and 'What have you tried?' The answers reveal real pain, not hypothetical interest. TinyMVP is built on this principle ‚Äî instead of surveys, it gives people a way to put money down. That's the ultimate Mom Test.

üöÄ How I Started

Noticed the pattern

In every indie hacker community, the same post: 'I spent 3 weeks on my landing page.' Meanwhile, the idea was untested. I thought: what if the landing page took 5 minutes?

Built for one person

A friend had a SaaS idea. I built her a one-page site with a Stripe button in an afternoon. She got 3 paying customers in 48 hours. That was the signal.

Made it repeatable

Turned the one-off into a template system. Pick a layout, fill in 4 fields, connect Stripe, deploy. Under 5 minutes for anyone.

Shared in communities

Posted in 3 indie hacker Discord servers. 200 signups in the first week. Most valuable feedback: people wanted custom domains, which I hadn't even considered.

üí° Tips & Lessons Learned

Charge from day one

Even $1 filters out people who'd never pay. Free users give you vanity metrics. Paying users give you real signal. Charge from day one.

Your landing page is your MVP

Don't build the product, then build a landing page for it. The landing page IS the MVP. If people won't sign up for the page, they won't use the product.

Constraints breed creativity

I limited TinyMVP to 4 input fields on purpose. Every field I removed forced a clearer value proposition from the user.

Community distribution > SEO

For early-stage projects, posting in 3 relevant communities beats months of SEO work. Go where your users already hang out.
